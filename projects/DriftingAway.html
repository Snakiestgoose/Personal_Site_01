Drifting Away

Project Type Game Jam
Software Used Unity
Languages Used C#
Primary Role(s) Game Programming and Design

Overview

Drifting Away is a typing, arcade style game that my wife and I created for the Melrose Game Jam 2019 (The theme was dreams). My wife helped with the music and original design, and I did the programming, developing, and etc..
The game has two core mechanics through two different game modes. The first is the awake state where the player types the falling words as the come across the screen. The scripts detect which word the player presses by comparing the words on screen to the letter the player has pressed. Once a word is selected, the player must finish that word before moving to the next. If the player lets the words get to the bottom, then it's game over.
In the dream world, the layer is now a 2D sprite who walks around a fantasy village. I only created one interaction with a wizard character who teaches the player how to use their magic. When fighting the player can hit space to enter magic mode and known spells appear at the bottom. The player must type the spell to use it to hit the aproaching enemies before they reach the town.
There are two modes to choose from the menu, one that is a story in which the player types through the diary of the character in the awake setting, and then plays through a sequence in the dream setting. Alternatively the player can choose to do an arcade mode of either setting and play endlessly.
